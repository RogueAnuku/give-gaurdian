Give Guardian

In the course of our assignment, we envisioned a groundbreaking web-browser extension tailored to empower philanthropists, varify charities, and organizations alike. Leveraging cutting-edge AI authentication, we offer a streamlined process for these entities to secure verified accounts, fostering trust and authenticity.

The team: Anika McPhee (User Research), Dhwani Trivedi (Coding), Chiara Fallani (User Interface), Desiree Henry (User Interface)

The motive behind charitable giving is noble, yet certain individuals exploit the kindness of others. As a donor, it is important to know for certain the legitimacy of the organizations that you choose to support.

Enter our web-browser extension concept, Give Guardian, designed to easily verify Not-for-profit organizations. With Give Guardian we aim to deliver a seamless donations experience, promote financial transparency, and foster connections with charitable causes. Through our AI-powered authentication process, these organizations and charities can establish verified accounts, trust and authenticity. Ensuring that the intentions behind every act of giving are genuinely met.

Empathy

One of the techniques we deployed to better understand the users was the creation of our user persona

Our target demographic focuses on adults aged 61-71, as secondary research indicates that this age group is the most inclined to contribute.

Test

We had five participants partake in a moderated usability test. The testing was conducted in August 2023.

  • #1 Have the user open the Give Guardian extension to check for authenticity
  • #2 Have the participant use filter functions.
  • #3 Have the user navigate and provide feedback on the Learn More feature.
  • #4 Request for the user to find and utilize the 'compare' feature

Conflicts

Out of the four tasks and five users only one particpant struggled. 4/5 Participants found the designs intuitive and easy to completed the task without hesitation. One person struggled to understand the page that provides a break down in the charity .

Our users were confused by inconsistent iconography. We had a heart icon and a save icon.

“I enjoy the compare feature. I would, however, want to see the content spread out, it seems a bit busy."-Participant

Resolutons

In our usability test, a user had automatically assumed all contents on the page below were related to finances. So, for the high- fedility prototype we structured the elements with colors to give them additional definition.
